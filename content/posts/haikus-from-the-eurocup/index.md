Something very strange happened during the [EuroCup 2012](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012). Something poetic.
![EuroCup 2012](http://hishanghai.info/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/euro-cup-2012.jpg)As giants in the world of [European football](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_football "European football") faced off against each other in one of the -- in my opinion -- most dramatic tournaments in recent sports history, for some reason, the community banter on [ESPN](http://espn.go.com/ "ESPN")'s online stats and live chat widget ([ESPN GameCast](http://espn.go.com "ESPN.com")), began producing a steady stream of [haikus](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haiku "Haiku").
Why did this happen? What does it mean? Can something interesting be said here about the population of fans that watch soccer on ESPN Gamecast? Perhaps if I watched GameCast more frequently, I might know how unusual (or usual) this sort of thing really is... but I don't. Do fans of other sports emit random bouts of poetry?
I'll leave all these questions to you, but I will say that it brightened my day in a most unexpected way.
