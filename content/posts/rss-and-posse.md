This blog is setup with POSSE in mind -- the idea that you can Publish on
your Own Site, and then Syndicate Elsewhere. It's a principle that comes
from the IndieWeb community. One can own one's own content, and keep it under
control, even if platforms (like Linkedin, or the Fediverse) are utilized 
to distribute and share this content. 

RSS is also important. RSS is to consuming content what POSSE is to
publishing it. Both are about retaining ownership, staying interoperable,
keeping switching costs low. Setting up my site with RSS paves a way
for those who want to read my content to do so on their own terms. 

The ideas that I have in mind are as follows. POSSE, as noted already.
Decentralized is good. Consider the protocol, and only then be about the
application or product. My presence and usage of a platform or application
is a vote of confidence, and affects more than just me, so I should be
intentional to make choices that favor those with fewer choices.
Privacy matters. Moderation matters. 

Today, most people understand why these things matter. Why many might not
trust companies like facebook or twitter, or why they would want to 
avoid entrusting their content to a platform that isn't open and free 
("free" as in freedom, not as in gratis). 

To the extent I'm using platforms right now, they are Mastodon and
Linkedin. The former isn't an addiction or a significant timesink, yet. I
have bridges setup to, for example, blusky. Linkedin is a necessary evil,
and any posts will be reposts for syndication. I pay for a nice RSS Reader
experience: Readwise Reader, which has reasonable allowances for
portability.
