In a conversation with Louis Potok in 2021, Louis suggested including a
Wall of Thanks to this website. What a great idea! 

I looked at several sites for inspiration, starting with the WattTime team's [Wall of
Fame](https://web.archive.org/web/20231114013708/https://watttime.org/about/wall-of-fame/)
(since removed). Each had their own take on the Wall, their own ideas of
who to include and why. 

But when I sat down to write, it felt like a terrible idea! Why would I
volunteer to give what equates to a bad acceptance speech for a not-award?
No one cares, it will surely be way too long, and inevitably someone will
feel left out. 

A few years went by, and occasionally I scribbled down a note. Recently I
wrote to myself: "Don't overthink it!". Then I finally made the damned thing. 

If nothing else, the process was valuable as a reflection. I reached out to 
reconnect with some great folk. 

The list of people on the Wall is not exhaustive, not trying to be. I
scoped it down to my more-recent work-life, and kept some of it merely
illustrative. I'm happy with how it turned out, for now, and I can always
change it up. 

As of today, when this post was written, the introduction to the Wall reads: 
> Thank you to all the many advisors, thought partners, family, colleagues
> and friends that have supported me and influenced my ideas through the
> years.

You can view the page at [/wall-of-thanks/](/wall-of-thanks).
