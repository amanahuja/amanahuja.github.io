In a conversation with Louis Potok in 2021, Louis suggested including a
Wall of Thanks to this website. What a great idea! 

I looked at several sites for inspiration, starting with the WattTime team's [Wall of
Fame](https://web.archive.org/web/20231114013708/https://watttime.org/about/wall-of-fame/)
(since removed). Each had their own take on the Wall, their own ideas of
who to include and why. 

I sat down to write one. Now it felt like a terrible idea! Why would I
volunteer to give a bad acceptance speech? No one cares, it will surely go
on for way too long, and inevitably someone will feel left out. 

A few years went by as I waffled over the decision, occasionally scribbling
down a note. Recently I wrote: "Don't overthink it!", and then made the
damned thing. 

If nothing else, the process was valuable. It got long, and then short. The
list of people is not exhaustive; the intention is to be illustrative and
limit scope to my work-life. I'm happy with how it turned out, and
definitely plan to change it frequently. 

The page's introduction (as of today) reads: 
> Thank you to all the many advisors, thought partners, family, colleagues
> and friends that have supported me and influenced my ideas through the
> years.
