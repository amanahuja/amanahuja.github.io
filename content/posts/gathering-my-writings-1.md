Although this section of the site is new, there are many posts and papers
that I've written elsewhere. It's content sitting on other platforms. 
I'd like to gather some of that stuff and bring it here. 

I [posted earlier about why that might be
important](/posts/rss-and-posse) -- but simply put, I'd like to make some
disperse content easier to find, to show continuity, to be able to observe
my ideas and my writing style evolve over time, and to have ownership over
what what I've written. 

---

One of the old collections I plan to migrate over was called the Pafnuty
blog. 

From 2008 to 2014, I kept a blog called Pafnuty, hosted on WordPress.com.
Wordpress is an amazing platform, and I probably used it for more than a
dozen significant websites and projects. 

The blog had no particular focus. There are notes from what I was reading
at the time, small essays, journal entries, descriptions of projects. There
are digressions about music, food, travel. Many of these posts were short. 

I am a little embarrassed about many of the posts, and my writing style has
changed, and so forth. But for the most part these posts hold up better
than I expected, as things I wrote long ago. 

The plan isn't to move everything. I've filtered them down, removed most of
the ones that were just links, or ones that don't make sense anymore: if
they rely strongly on content that doesn't exist at the provided link, for
instance. As of the time of writing, there are 81 posts, but I figure some
of those will be trimmed out as well, leaving perhaps 60-70 in the end. 

--- 

If you write on the web and you haven't thought about where your words
live, it might be worth your consideration. The case for owning your
content was always good; it's only gotten more obvious.
